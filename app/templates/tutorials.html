Push-Up Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Start in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to toe.
  3. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
  4. Push back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
Key Techniques:
  • Keep your core engaged throughout the movement.
  • Don't let your hips sag or lift too high.
  • Breathe out as you push up, breathe in as you lower down.
Variations:
  • Knee push-ups (easier)
  • Incline push-ups (easier)
  • Decline push-ups (harder)
  • Diamond push-ups (harder, more focus on triceps)
Common Mistakes:
  • Dropping the hips or arching the back
  • Not going through the full range of motion
  • Flaring the elbows too far out
  • Looking up or down instead of keeping the neck neutral
Squat Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Stand with feet slightly wider than hip-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  2. Keep your chest up and core engaged.
  3. Bend at the knees and hips, lowering as if sitting in a chair.
  4. Lower until thighs are at least parallel to the ground.
  5. Push through your heels to return to the starting position.
Key Techniques:
  • Keep your knees tracking in line with your toes.
  • Maintain weight in your heels and mid-foot.
  • Keep your back straight and chest up.
Variations:
  • Box squats (controlled depth)
  • Goblet squats (with weight)
  • Sumo squats (wider stance)
  • Jump squats (plyometric)
Common Mistakes:
  • Letting knees cave inward
  • Rising onto toes during the movement
  • Rounding the lower back
  • Not going deep enough
Jumping Jack Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Stand upright with your feet together and arms at your sides.
  2. Jump up, spreading your legs shoulder-width apart while raising your arms overhead.
  3. Quickly return to the starting position by jumping again.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
Key Techniques:
  • Maintain a steady rhythm throughout the exercise.
  • Land softly on the balls of your feet.
  • Engage your core to stabilize your movements.
Variations:
  • Low-Impact Jumping Jacks (step out instead of jumping).
  • Cross Jacks (cross arms and legs while jumping).
  • Star Jumps (explosive jump with arms and legs fully extended).
  • Squat Jacks (combine a squat with the jumping jack movement).
Common Mistakes:
  • Landing heavily on your heels instead of softly on the balls of your feet.
  • Not fully extending your arms or legs during the movement.
  • Slouching or not keeping your back straight.
  • Moving too fast and losing control of form.
Hamstring Stretch Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Stand upright with your feet hip-width apart.
  2. Step one foot forward and keep the other leg straight.
  3. Hinge at your hips and lower your torso toward the extended leg.
  4. Hold the stretch for 20-30 seconds, then switch legs.
Key Techniques:
  • Keep your back straight while bending forward.
  • Engage your core to maintain balance.
  • Do not lock your knees; keep them slightly bent.
Variations:
  • Seated Hamstring Stretch
  • Standing Hamstring Stretch with a Chair
  • Dynamic Hamstring Stretch (leg swings)
Common Mistakes:
  • Rounding your back instead of hinging at the hips.
  • Locking your knees, which can cause strain.
  • Bouncing during the stretch instead of holding it steadily.
Tree Pose Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Stand upright with your feet together and arms at your sides.
  2. Shift your weight onto one foot and place the sole of the other foot on your inner thigh or calf (avoid the knee).
  3. Bring your palms together in front of your chest or raise them overhead.
  4. Hold the pose for 20-30 seconds, then switch sides.
Key Techniques:
  • Engage your core to maintain balance.
  • Keep your standing leg straight but not locked.
  • Focus your gaze on a fixed point to help with stability.
Variations:
  • Tree Pose with Hands on Hips
  • Tree Pose with Arms Extended Overhead
  • Tree Pose with a Wall for Support
Common Mistakes:
  • Placing the foot on the knee, which can cause strain.
  • Leaning to one side instead of staying upright.
  • Not engaging the core, leading to instability.
Plank Tutorial
Instructions:
  1. Start in a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders.
  2. Engage your core and keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Hold the position for the desired amount of time.
Key Techniques:
  • Keep your core tight and avoid letting your hips sag or rise too high.
  • Keep your neck neutral by looking slightly ahead of your hands.
  • Breathe steadily throughout the exercise.
Variations:
  • Forearm Plank
  • Side Plank
  • Plank with Shoulder Taps
  • Plank to Push-Up
Common Mistakes:
  • Letting the hips sag or rise too high.
  • Not engaging the core, leading to poor form.
  • Holding your breath instead of breathing steadily.
